Essential Oils 101

Essential Oils 101

Essential Oils 101 is a landing page to assist customers who are new to essential oils to gain a broader understanding of the ecosystem. Customers gain a quick overview of all relevant products and their use.

This is a shortened desktop example of the page.

The challenge

Never before has Earthbound considered the need to inform their customers online about their Essential Oil product line. Customers would call Customer Service to ask questions about what they are and how to use them and the problems they had with oils. It got a little concerning to find out that customers were misusing oils that led to health concerns, and also product returns.

Another problem arose from doing a casual survey of employees to find out how much they knew about the product line. It was surprising to discover even employees who were around the product line, whether they were selling it in stores or fulfilling orders did not have product knowledge.

An uninformed employee culture resulted in the miscategorization of these products online that led to confused and unhappy customers.

We used these insights from user feedback and analytics to help inform a new landing page.

Research and discovery

It wasn't until my team put together the first UX Testing Lab that we discovered just how profound the lack of knowledge about these products was from our employees (who were our testers).

The truth of the matter was that our testers did not know the difference between our Fragrance Oils, which are chemicals, and our natural Essential Oils, much less how to safely use and apply both oils.

The only way to decipher any significance between both types of oils was by separating each into their respective categories in the main navigation. However, we intercepted more confusion about knowing which oils and accessories go together; fragrance oils can only be used with oil burners while essential oils can only be used with diffusers. How could we tie together the right accessories with the right oils while keeping our categories focused?

We wanted the Essential Oils landing page to be simple and informative for customers who lack product knowledge, but also the shortest journey for knowledgeable shoppers to jump quickly to find what they want.

Sketching wireframes

After researching how competitors inform their customers about Essential Oils, we gathered information from the product buyer and talked to the manufacturer about their products. I sketched out wireframes for the Landing page, the product page, and the journey from our two primary access points: the homepage and the main navigation.

Identifying and maintaining the product's truth through the development cycle was a wicked challenge.paraphrased from Devin Moore

Stakeholders were concerned about liability; what we should claim to know about product safety and application. It was a challenge to find the right language and design that could help customers get a broad view of the facets of our oils and its accessories while also not being too explicit about safety concerns.

I started planning the build while the designer branded the mockup, gathered images, and rough content from the content manager.

Our initial goal was to get a basic version up and working so I could begin writing out user stories and prioritize them for development.

FAQs

The concern to inform, but to not be the authority on the safety of essential oils led to the use of FAQs. In this format, the information could be dry and resourceful. We took our research on the most commonly asked questions from social sites and from speaking directly with the leading distributor of essential oils, Wyndmere Naturals.

Carousels

First and foremost this page is for shopping, which means we need to include products. Customers who are new to essential oils get exposure to various products through the carousels.
